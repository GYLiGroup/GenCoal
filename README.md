# GenCoal
GenCoal has two algorithms that are ClipIRMol and structural evolution for generating coal molecular models. Input FT-IR to generate SMILES of Coal fragments based on deep learning model ClipIRMol. Feeding your experimental <sup>13</sup>C NMR and elemental ratios to generate final coal model using structral based on structural evolution.

# Coal Macromolecular Model Construction Using Deep Learning

## Description

Recent advancements in the field of coal chemistry have been made by the Coal Chemical Engineering research group led by Professor Guangyue Li at North China University of Science and Technology. Coal continues to play a significant role in the energy sector. The construction of molecular structure models for coal is crucial for researching combustion, gasification, liquefaction, and other reaction processes. However, the amorphous structure of coal, with its complex chemical composition and structural morphology, presents a significant challenge. Coal exhibits a wide variety of possible chemical configurations, and differences in coal rank further complicate the molecular structure.

Building on earlier research that established macromolecular models for coal and coke using characterization results and computational chemistry methods (Fuel, 2021, 300, 120981; Carbon, 2020, 162, 56), the team has now developed a method for constructing coal macromolecular models that align with experimental characterization outcomes. This method, leveraging inverse molecular design, deep learning technology, and quantum chemical calculations, significantly reduces the workload of spectrogram analysis while accurately identifying and analyzing coal's structural features.

The ClipIRMol program, developed by the team, allows for the rapid construction of model molecules containing thousands of atoms within minutes. These models serve as initial structures for quantum chemical calculations and molecular simulations, propelling research into the molecular structural changes during coal pyrolysis and related reactions.

This coal macromolecular model construction system introduces deep learning technology to the field of coal chemistry, offering new research methods and perspectives. Additionally, the approach can be extended to the structural model construction of oil shale, lignin, and other amorphous organic macromolecules, demonstrating broad application potential and value.

This groundbreaking work, titled "Construction of macromolecular model of coal based on deep learning algorithm," was recently published in the journal *Energy* with master's student Haodong Liu as the first author, and Associate Professor Hang Zhang and Professor Guangyue Li as corresponding authors.

The research was supported by the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the Natural Science Foundation of Hebei Province, and key funds from North China University of Science and Technology.
